Lesson Plan

Title Symbolic Culture Vessels
Designers Chrisitna Luby and Karen Keller
Summary Students are challenged to construct a vessel through the use of CAD programs that will symbolically represent aspects culture. Students will participate in group discussions focused on aspects of culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion as it pertains to their identity.
Grade Level 7 & 8
Key Words
  • Culture- The set of shared attitudes, values, goals, practices, customary beliefs, social forms, and material traits of a racial, religious, or social group.
  • Ethnicity- a particular ethnic affiliation or group
  • Heritage- background from which one comes, or any sort of inherited property or goods
  • Religion- the belief in and worship of a superhuman controlling power, especially a personal God or gods.
  • Symbols- a mark or character used as a conventional representation of an object, function, or process, e.g., the letter or letters standing for a chemical element or a character in musical notation.
Standards
  • 2.10 For shape, form, and pattern, use and be able to identify an expanding and increasingly sophisticated array of shapes and forms, such as organic, geometric, positive and negative, or varieties of symmetry.
  • 3.5 Create symbolic artwork by substituting symbols for objects, relationships, or ideas.
  • 5.6 Demonstrate the ability to describe the kinds of imagery used to represent subject matter and ideas, for example, literal representation, simplification, abstraction, or symbolism.
Objectives
  • Students will use a CAD software such as tinkercad to constructed a three dimensional vessel
  • I can use 3D software to construct solid forms
  • Students will use symbols to show identity and its relationship to culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion
  • I can create symbols to show culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion
  • Students will participate in peer discussions on the topics of culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion
  • I can verbally define culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion
  • I can explain how culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion impact my identity
