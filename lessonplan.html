Lesson Plan

Title Symbolic Culture Vessels
Designers Chrisitna Luby and Karen Keller
Essential Question What does it mean to belong?
Summary Students are challenged to construct a vessel through the use of CAD programs that will symbolically represent aspects culture. Students will participate in group discussions focused on aspects of culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion as it pertains to their identity.
Grade Level 7 & 8
Standards
  • 2.10 For shape, form, and pattern, use and be able to identify an expanding and increasingly sophisticated array of shapes and forms, such as organic, geometric, positive and negative, or varieties of symmetry.
  • 3.5 Create symbolic artwork by substituting symbols for objects, relationships, or ideas.
  • 5.6 Demonstrate the ability to describe the kinds of imagery used to represent subject matter and ideas, for example, literal representation, simplification, abstraction, or symbolism.
Objectives
  • Students will use a CAD software such as tinkercad to constructed a three dimensional vessel
    • I can use 3D software to construct solid forms
  • Students will use symbols to show identity and its relationship to culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion
    • I can create symbols to show culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion
  • Students will participate in peer discussions on the topics of culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion
    • I can verbally define culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion I can explain how culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion impact my identity
Overarching Goal Through the exploration of culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion, the class will investigate symbolic ways to communicate identities. By the end of this lesson students visually and verbally communicate the impact culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion have on their identity along with the formation of a community, through the creation of a 3D printed vessel
Guiding Questions
  • Where does my family come from?
  • What is culture, ethnicity, religion, and heritage?
  • How are culture, ethnicity, religion, and heritage different from each other?
  • Where does culture, ethnicity, religion, and heritage overlap in someone's identity?
  • How does our culture, ethnicity, religion, and heritage affect our community?
  • Is my identity shaped by my culture, ethnicity, religion, and heritage?
  • Does where you live or come from define your community?
Motivation
  • Introduction- think, pair, share what is culture?
    • Teacher will facilitate questioning using Visual Thinking Strategies to a powerpoint presentation featuring:
    • Multiple kinds of vessels, that display symbols relating to ethnicity, heritage, and religion
    • For each image shown the teacher will first say “Please look at the picture silently for a minute and think about what you see. What's going on in the picture?” after the minute the teacher will ask "What do you see in the image”
    • Teacher will ask students what symbols and images they see on the vessel
    • Teacher will ask students if they think the vessel communicated individual identity or a communal identity
  • Group discussion, prompted by the teachers questions:
    • What is the impact of ethnicity, heritage, and religion in communities? Does culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion have the ability to unite/ form a community?
    • Google arts and culture will be used to find vessels and symbols from different cultures
    Performance Task Students will be able to discuss and understand the impact of aspects like ethnicity, heritage, religion, and culture on a community and an individual. Students are challenged to symbolically represent their culture through the construction of the form of a CAD file that will be 3D printed. Through group discussions and reflection students will understand the aspects of culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion as it pertains to their identity.
    Knowledge & Skills Knowledge:
    • Define culture, ethnicity, heritage, symbol, vessel, and religion
    • Identify ancestral location and culture can form identities and communities
    • Identify aspects of culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion
    Skills:
    • Build upon studio skills using 3D processes
    • Discuss aspects of ethnicity, heritage, and religion
    • Analyze the relationship between cultural heritage and community
    • Express thoughts and opinions, give evidence and/or reasoning
    • Continue to build upon visual thinking strategies

    Instructional Approach Multicultural: Through exploration of culture, ethnicity, heritage, and religion, the teacher will prompt discussions on what it means to belong and be a member of multiple cultures
    Key Words
    • Culture- The set of shared attitudes, values, goals, practices, customary beliefs, social forms, and material traits of a racial, religious, or social group.
    • Ethnicity- a particular ethnic affiliation or group
    • Heritage- background from which one comes, or any sort of inherited property or goods
    • Religion- the belief in and worship of a superhuman controlling power, especially a personal God or gods.
    • Symbols- a mark or character used as a conventional representation of an object, function, or process, e.g., the letter or letters standing for a chemical element or a character in musical notation.
