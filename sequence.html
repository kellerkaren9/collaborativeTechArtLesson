Sequence of Actiivties

Class #1

  • Introduction to the lesson
  • The lesson will begin with the teacher asking students Where does their family come from? (10 min)
  • Students are asked to think, pair, share their thoughts on culture, ethnicity, religion, and heritage, along with the definition/meaning (5 min).
  • Students will be able to find definitions of these terms on the wall at the front of the classroom.
  • Teacher will ask guiding questions
  • Students will engage in small group discussion of guiding questions explaining their thoughts and opinions. Students will be tasked to provide evidence and reasoning for their thoughts and opinions. Teacher will prompt this discussion by reading guiding questions out loud, and asking students to share their answers in their groups (10 min)
  • Teacher will engage students by using visual teaching strategies. For each image shown the teacher will first say “Please look at the picture silently for a minute and think about what you see. What's going on in the picture?” after the minute the teacher will ask "What do you see in the image” (10 min)
  • Teacher will ask students what they think a symbol is/ give definitions
  • Teacher will show powerpoint examples(10 min)
  • Teacher will pass out paper to students and ask them to draw symbols that show their culture, ethnicity, religion, and heritage (15 min)

    • Class #2

  • will ask students to do a think pair share with google arts and culture. Each student will have 5 minutes to find a vessel on the site that they like, then 2 minutes to discuss with the person next to them, then each pair shares their selections and reasons for choosing them with the class.
  • will tell students that this class they will be designing their vessel, showing their culture.
  • Teacher will instruct students to continue to sketch out possible vessel shapes and symbol designs

    • Class #3

  • Teacher will tell students that they are going to explore the tinkercad software today and learn its functions
  • will demo various techniques on tinkercad as students follow along on computers
  • Teacher will ask students for examples of shapes and forms. the teacher will show students examples CAD files that utilize asymmetrical and symmetrical forms along with geometric and organic forms
  • Students will use the rest of class to continue to explore how to use tinkercad

    • Classes #4-5

  • Teacher will tell students that today they will be using their sketches as reference to create their 3-D vessel in tinkercad
  • Teacher will roam class to help students individually

    • Class #6

  • Teacher will instruct students that today they will be preparing the files and 3D printing them
  • Teacher will demonstrate how to prepare a file for 3D printing
  • Students will prepare their own 3D file and will download it onto the computer hooked up to the 3D printer
  • Students will be given a worksheet to complete when file is loaded and ready to print
  • Teacher will print files after class

    • Class #7

  • Critique
  • Teacher will reference daily agenda and tell students that today will be a critique day
  • Teacher will ask students what they know about critiques and what they think a critique is and students will answer
  • Teacher will ask students how they think they should act during a critique
  • Students will answer
  • Teacher will tell students that during a critique they should
    • Be quiet when someone else is talking
    • Be positive when talking about someone else's work
    • Give constructive criticism
    • Raise their hand if they have anything to say about anyone else's work
  • Teacher will pass out students finished ceramic vessels
  • Teacher will ask if any students would like to start, if no students volunteer teacher will call on either student at the end of the the U shaped table
  • Teacher will ask student “What parts of your identity did you show? What is your favorite symbol you made?
  • Student will respond
  • Teacher will wait for other students questions or comments
  • Teacher will continue this process for all students
